# Banff 2022 Diagnostic Categories (Table 3)

## Category 1: Normal Biopsy or Nonspecific Changes
Requires exclusion of any diagnosis from Banff Diagnostic Categories 2-4, 6 below.

## Category 2: Acute T-Cell Mediated Rejection (TCMR)

### Acute TCMR Grade IA
- **Criteria**: i ≥ 1, t ≥ 1, v = 0
- **Interpretation**: Mild acute TCMR with interstitial inflammation and tubulitis

### Acute TCMR Grade IB
- **Criteria**: i ≥ 1, t ≥ 1, v = 0 (with more extensive involvement than IA)
- **Interpretation**: Moderate acute TCMR

### Acute TCMR Grade IIA
- **Criteria**: v ≥ 1 (with or without i/t involvement)
- **Interpretation**: Acute TCMR with mild to moderate intimal arteritis

### Acute TCMR Grade IIB
- **Criteria**: v ≥ 1 (more extensive than IIA)
- **Interpretation**: Acute TCMR with moderate to severe intimal arteritis

### Acute TCMR Grade III
- **Criteria**: Transmural arteritis and/or fibrinoid necrosis of arteries
- **Interpretation**: Severe acute TCMR with arterial involvement

## Category 2: Antibody-Mediated Rejection (AMR)

### Active AMR
- **Criteria**: Microvascular inflammation (g ≥ 1 and/or ptc ≥ 1) + C4d ≥ 2 + DSA positive
- **Interpretation**: Active antibody-mediated rejection with complement activation

### Chronic Active AMR
- **Criteria**: Chronic lesions (cg ≥ 1, cv ≥ 1, ci ≥ 1, ct ≥ 1) + C4d ≥ 2 + DSA positive
- **Interpretation**: Chronic active antibody-mediated rejection

## Category 3: Borderline Changes (Suspicious for Acute TCMR)
- **Criteria**: i ≥ 1, t = 0 OR i = 0, t ≥ 1 (without v involvement)
- **Interpretation**: Borderline changes that may represent early TCMR

## Category 4: Chronic Active T-Cell Mediated Rejection
- **Criteria**: i-IFTA ≥ 2 + t-IFTA ≥ 1 (in areas of interstitial fibrosis)
- **Interpretation**: Chronic active TCMR with inflammation in areas of fibrosis

## Category 5: Chronic Allograft Nephropathy
- **Criteria**: ci ≥ 1, ct ≥ 1 (without evidence of acute rejection)
- **Interpretation**: Chronic changes without active rejection

## Category 6: Other Diagnoses
- BK-Virus Nephropathy
- Acute Tubular Necrosis (ATN)
- Thrombotic Microangiopathy (TMA)
- Glomerulonephritis
- Recurrent/De Novo Disease
